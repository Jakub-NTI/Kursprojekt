The Creation Process

Your first step in playing an adventurer in the Dungeons & Dragons game is to imagine and create a character of your own. Your character is a combination of game statistics, roleplaying hooks, and your imagination. You choose a race (such as human or halfling) and a class (such as fighter or wizard). You also invent the personality, appearance, and backstory of your character. Once completed, your character serves as your representative in the game, your avatar in the Dungeons & Dragons world.

But before you click that "Create Character" button, think about the kind of adventurer you want to play. ou might be a courageous fighter, a skulking rogue, a fervent cleric, or a flamboyant wizard. Or you might be more interested in an unconventional character, such as a brawny rogue who likes hand-to-hand combat, or a sharpshooter who picks off enemies from afar. Do you like fantasy fiction featuring dwarves or elves? Try building a character of one of those races. Do you want your character to be the toughest adventurer at the table? Consider the fighter class. If you don’t know where else to begin, take a look at the illustrations in any Dungeons & Dragons book to see what catches your interest.

Once you have a character in mind, click the big green button, read our selection or rules and follow the steps for creating your character, making decisions that reflect the character you want. Your conception of your character might evolve with each choice you make. What’s important is that you come to the table with a character you’re excited to play.

About

D&D Character Creator is a webproject by a swedish highschool student who wanted to make a easy way to make characters for the TTRPG game Duneons and Dragons while working for school at the same time. This site works as a easy way for D&D fans to find information regarding character creation and create.

Disclaimer

This website is meant for people who already own all the books where the information on this site is gathered from so that they can easily access that information and create characters. It is also not meant for players who are completely new to D&D, with many important details about gameplay left out to effectivaly gather all nessessary information for efficeient character creation.
