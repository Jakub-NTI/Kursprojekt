Level

Before you can start creating a character you'll have to choose what level the character will be. This information is told to the players before the campaign starts and tells you how powerfull you are and what new abilities you start with. Campaigns usually start at levels 1-5, with level 1, 3 and 5 capaigns being the most common.

Rules

There are many rules that Wizards of the Coast released during the past few years. This character creator uses information from the following books for a balanced character making experience where the player has the freedom they need to make the character they want to play with:

The Player’s Handbook is the essential reference for every Dungeons & Dragons roleplayer. It contains rules for character creation and advancement, backgrounds and skills, exploration and combat, equipment, spells, and much more. It contains 12 of the 13 classes along with several base subclasses and the 9 most common races along with their subraces.

The wizard Tasha, whose great works include the spell Tasha’s hideous laughter, has gathered bits and bobs of precious lore during her illustrious career as an adventurer. Tasha's Cauldron of Everythig is one of the expansion books with the most new options for character creation ever made. It contains several subclasses and alternate rules important for character creation. It is also the only book that has new subclass options for the Artificer.

The beholder Xanathar—Waterdeep’s most infamous crime lord—is known to hoard information on friend and foe alike. Xanathar's Guid to Everything contains 25 additional subclasses for the classes from the Players Handbook.

Compiling and updating monsters that originally appeared in Volo’s Guide to Monsters and Mordenkainen’s Tome of Foes, Mordenkainen Presents the Monsters of the Multiverse includes over 30 playable races. Bringing all the game’s setting-agnostic races into one book, complementing the races in the Player’s Handbook.

Althrough Ebberon: Rising from the Last War mainly focuses on the story of Ebberon, a war-torn world filled with magic-fueled technology, airships and lightning trains. It also icludes the Artificer class and the Warforged race for player use.

Focusing on the world of Ravnica from Magic: the gathering, Guildmaster's Guide to Ravnica brings a few playable races for players to use such as the Loxodon, Simic Hybrid and Vedalken.

Being from the Spelljammer: Adventures in Space trilogy, the Astral Adventures Guide is the book that focuses on player options, bringing a few unusual but interesting races for the player to use. These races include the Plasmoid, Hadozee and Thri-Kreen.

Another adventure in one of the worlds of Magic: the gathering, Mythic Odysseys of Thereos adds some ancient-greece-themed character options. It includes the Leonin, Satyr and Minotaur races and the Athlete background.

Van Richten's Guide to Ravenloft is focused on the world of Ravenloft, the mist-shrouded lands where infamous Darklords lurk among ageless vampires, zombie hordes, cosmic terrors, and worse. It comes with the Dhampir, Hexblood and Reborn races along with the the College of Spirits Bard and the Undead Warlock subclasses.

From the jungles of Chult, these tribal frog-like humanoids are fiercely territorial and naturally secrete poison to use as weapons... The One Grund Above rules supplement simply adds the Grung race to the table.
